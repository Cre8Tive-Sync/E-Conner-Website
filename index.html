Municipal Profile
About Conner
History
Named after an American Engineer
Conner is a 2nd-class municipality in the province of Apayao, Cordillera Administrative Region. It was named after Norman Conner, an American engineer who oversaw the construction of the main roads that effectively ended the isolation of Apayao from the outside world during the early American era.
Vision & Mission
Our Commitment
"A progressive, self-reliant, and empowered Municipality of Conner with God-fearing, peace-loving, and law-abiding citizens."

Nature · Culture · Heritage
🏔️
Apayao-Abulug River
A majestic river flowing through Conner, part of the UNESCO-listed Apayao Biosphere Reserve.
💧
Natural Springs
Cool, clear springs nestled in the mountains of Conner, accessible via scenic forest trails.
🌳
Dipterocarp Forest
Home to lawaan trees, Rafflesia flowers, and critically endangered wildlife of the Cordillera.
Indigenous Heritage
The Isnag People
The Isnag are the indigenous people of Apayao. In Conner, the Nabwangan dialect is spoken. Their rich oral traditions, weaving arts, and ancestral practices are preserved across the municipality's upland communities.
